

Topic 1: The Solar System (basic astronomy)
------------------------------------------
The Solar System has eight planets: Mercury, Venus, Earth, Mars, Jupiter, Saturn,
Uranus, and Neptune. Earth is the third planet from the Sun and the only known
planet to support life. Mars is often called the "Red Planet" because iron oxide
in its soil gives it a reddish appearance.

Jupiter is the largest planet in the Solar System. It is a gas giant with many
moons; one of its most famous moons is Europa. Saturn is known for its prominent
rings, which are made mostly of ice and rock particles. Neptune is very far from
the Sun and has strong winds in its atmosphere.

Topic 3: Cooking notes (procedural instructions)
------------------------------------------------
To make lemon rice:
1) Cook 1 cup of rice and let it cool.
2) Heat 1 tablespoon oil in a pan.
3) Add mustard seeds, curry leaves, and peanuts (optional).
4) Add turmeric and salt.
5) Mix the cooked rice with the seasoning.
6) Finish with lemon juice and chopped coriander.

Important: if the rice is too hot when mixed, it may become mushy.

Topic 5: Databases and vector search (RAG-relevant definitions)
--------------------------------------------------------------
A database is a system that stores and organizes data so it can be retrieved and
updated efficiently. Relational databases (like PostgreSQL and MySQL) store data
in tables and often use SQL for queries. NoSQL databases (like MongoDB) store data
in flexible formats, such as documents.

A vector database stores embeddings (vectors) and is optimized for similarity
search. In many AI applications, text is converted into embeddings, and the system
retrieves the most semantically similar chunks to a user query.

Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) combines retrieval and generation:
first retrieve relevant context from an external knowledge base, then generate an
answer using an LLM conditioned on that retrieved context.
