In a distant city, a developer built a chatbot that remembered long conversations.
The chatbot used embeddings to turn text into vectors, and a vector store to quickly find similar chunks.
Whenever the user asked a question, the system retrieved the most relevant pieces of text and used them as context.

On rainy evenings, the developer sipped tea and refactored the code.
They replaced simple keyword search with semantic search.
Slowly, the answers became more accurate, and users started trusting the assistant.
